Explore the Planets

Mercury

The smallest and fastest planet, experiencing extreme heat during the day and freezing cold at night.

Venus

A scorching world with thick, toxic clouds, making it the hottest planet in the solar system.

Earth

The only known planet to support life, covered in vast oceans and a protective atmosphere.

Mars

The Red Planet, once home to flowing water, with the tallest volcano in the solar system.

Jupiter

The largest planet, a gas giant with powerful storms, including the massive Great Red Spot.

Saturn

Famous for its stunning rings, it is the least dense planet and could float on water.

Uranus

An icy blue giant that rotates on its side, making it unique among the planets.

Neptune

The farthest planet, known for its deep blue color and the fastest winds in the solar system.

Famous Space Missions

Chandrayaan-1

Launched on October 22, 2008, Chandrayaan-1 was India's first lunar mission. It discovered water molecules in the lunar soil and mapped the Moon's surface, contributing to global lunar science.

Mangalyaan (MOM)

Launched on November 5, 2013, the Mars Orbiter Mission made India the first Asian nation to reach Mars orbit on its first attempt. It studied the Martian atmosphere and surface features.

Chandrayaan-2

Launched on July 22, 2019, Chandrayaan-2 aimed to explore the Moon's south pole. Although the lander failed to soft-land, the orbiter continues to send valuable data about the lunar surface.

Chandrayaan-3

Launched on July 14, 2023, Chandrayaan-3 achieved a historic soft landing on the Moon's south pole on August 23, 2023, making India the first nation to land in that region.

Aditya-L1

Launched on September 2, 2023, Aditya-L1 is India's first solar observatory. Positioned at the L1 Lagrange point, it studies the Sun's corona, solar flares, and space weather impacts.
